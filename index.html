    About Ozone

    Ozone, normally known as O3, is present in the statosphere.
    It is a pale blue gas with a distinctively pungent smell.
    The function of the ozone layer is to shield the Earth
    from the harmful ultraviolet rays of the sun and to keep the Earth warm.
    These are the basic things about ozone. But for those curious ones out there,
    Here's a fun fact. Ozone alone explains the blue color of the sky.
    Ozone is a pale blue gas but in the lower stratosphere, where ozone layer is situated, and where it is very cold.

    Ozone Hole ðŸ˜±


    The ozone hole is not actually a hole in the ozone layer,
    but a region of depleted ozone in the stratosphere over the Antarctic. In recent years, the ozone depletion has also been noted in the northern polar region.
    The phenomenon is called ozone hole. chemicals called CFCs (chloroflourocarbons) are responsible for the hole in the ozone layer
    scientists estimate 80% of CFCs in the stratosphere are from humans, not nature.
    These chemicals escape from refrigeration and
    propellant devices and processes, such as refrigerators, air conditioners etc. They do not easily degenerate.
    In fact, CFCs can linger in the lower atmosphere for decades, and allows some of the chemicals to reach the stratosphere.
    Where, ultraviolet light breaks down the bond holding chlorine atoms to the CFC
    molecule, and the free chlorine atoms are able to participate many chemical reactions, which destroy ozone.

    Helping Ozone


    How can we protect the ozone layer?

    • Avoid gases dangerous to the ozone layer, due to their content or manufacturing process.
      Some of the most dangerous gases are CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons),methyl bromide and nitrous oxide.
    • Minimize the use of cars. The best transport option is urban, bicycle, or walking.
      If you use a car try to carpool with others in order to pollute less and save the environment.
    • Do not use cleaning products that are harmful to the environment and to us.
      replace these dangerous substances with non-toxic products such as vinegar or bicarbonate.
